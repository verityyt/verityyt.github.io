OpenRGB on a MSI MPG B550 Gaming Plus

(Fix from 2th April)

You have a MSI MPG B550 Gaming Plus and your Mainboard doesn't show up in the OpenRGB software? Here is a possible (working for me) solution for windows:

Example:

Step 1:

Uninstall the inpoutx64 driver, and uninstall OpenRGB by deleting the folder.

Step 1: Open registry → Press WIN + R and typ in regedit.exe and press ENTER

Step 2: Delete inpoutx64 entry in registery on the following path: Computer\HKEY_LOCAL_MACHINE\SYSTEM\CurrentControlSet\Services\

Step 4: Restart pc (After the reboot the driver is uninstalled)

Step 3: Now you have to delete the OpenRGB installation folder (Where ever you moved the folder)

Step 2:

Check if Mystic Light Controller is registered in your system.

Step 1: Download Zadig from (https://zadig.akeo.ie/)

Step 2: Launch the zadig.exe

Step 3: Enable List all devices in Options menu

Step 4: Check if the MYSTIC LIGHT (Mystic Light Controller) is found by Zadig

If MYSTIC LIGHT is found everything is fine and you can go on, if not try to enable system lights in your BIOS or somewhere and repeat Step 2-4.

Step 3:

Reinstall OpenRGB

Step 1: Download the latest OpenRGB build for your system (x64 or x84 | 64 or 32bit) from the master branch.

(https://gitlab.com/CalcProgrammer1/OpenRGB)

Step 2: Extract downloaded zip file

Step 3: Run OpenRGB.exe as administrator (to install inpoutx64 again)

Result:

Now you should have a device which is called MSI MYSTIC LIGHT, this is your new Mystic Light sectionand now you're able to configure all rgb sources off/on your mainboard.
